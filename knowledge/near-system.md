# NEAR.org Design System (extracted from live CSS, 2026)

NEAR'S COLOR SYSTEM: The base is pure black #000000 on pure white #FFFFFF with a warm paper cream #F2F1E9 for section breaks, then four flat accent hues wired into a page-theme system: signature green #00EC97, coral red #FF7966, periwinkle purple #9797FF, and teal blue #17D9D4, plus a soft mint #C7F5D8 used as a canvas background for generative art. Neutrals are named semantically in Tailwind (text-black-weak #757575, text-black-subtle #ACABA6, bg-white-weak #F9F9FA). The lesson: pick one loud brand hue per page via a body class (page-theme-green on the homepage) instead of scattering all accents everywhere, and keep every accent fully saturated and flat, never tinted or gradiented into mush.

NEAR'S TYPOGRAPHY: One custom grotesk carries the whole site: FKGrotesk, sans-serif, self-hosted as woff2 at weights 400 and 700 with font-display: swap, with Mona Sans as a secondary face. There is no serif, no second display font. Body text is 1rem/1.5. The lesson for builders: a single characterful grotesk plus a disciplined weight range (400 for giant display, 500 for headings) reads more premium than mixing families.

NEAR'S TYPE SCALE: Classes are named by role, not tag: .display-l is 3.5rem at weight 400 with line-height 1.07, scaling up to 5rem (80px) at line-height 1 on desktop; .display-m is 3rem to 4rem; .h1 is 2.5rem to 3rem; .h2 is 2rem weight 500 line-height 1.125, scaling to 2.75rem (44px) at line-height 1.09; .h3 is 1.5rem to 2.5rem; .h5 is 1.5rem weight 500 line-height 1.25. Crucially the HTML decouples semantics from size: the H3 subsection titles carry class h5 and the single H1 carries display-l with max-w-[850px] and text-pretty. The lesson: build a role-based scale, cap heading measure around 850px, and crush line-height toward 1.0 as size grows.

NEAR'S SHAPE AND RADIUS LANGUAGE: Corners stay tight and technical: the default .rounded is 0.25rem (4px), with 0.125rem and 0.5rem as the only other rectangle radii and 9999px reserved for pills. Buttons are quiet, mostly 1px border border-black outlines with rounded 4px corners and hover states like hover:md:bg-white-weak and a focus ring, with the loud version being bg-green with black text. The lesson: 4px radii plus hairline black borders on light backgrounds give a crisp engineering feel; avoid the 16px-plus soft-blob card look entirely.

NEAR'S MOTION SYSTEM: The workhorse easings are cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1) (Material standard) and cubic-bezier(0,0,.2,1) (decelerate) at short durations, 0.15s for hovers and 0.3s for most transitions, with 0.5s to 0.7s for larger reveals; carousel cards pop on hover with an overshoot spring cubic-bezier(.34,1.56,.64,1) at 0.4s and hover scales of 1.05 and 1.5. The signature move is a per-character wave: every letter of a link is a span with animation-delay: calc(n * 0.1s), and on hover it runs wave 2.5s ease-out infinite, nudging each character translateX(-0.5px) while tinting it to the page accent via --wave-color (for example #00EC97). The lesson: keep global motion fast and standard-eased, then spend the personality budget on one small, ownable text micro-animation.

NEAR'S GRADIENT AND TEXTURE RESTRAINT: There are almost no decorative gradients; the CSS only contains Tailwind directional fades (to right, to bottom, to bottom right) that run a flat accent like #00EC97 or #17D9D4 into transparent, used as edge fades and masks rather than backgrounds. Texture comes instead from a full-viewport grain.png overlay rendered at opacity 0.03, z-index 49, pointer-events-none over the entire page. The lesson: skip mesh gradients; a barely visible film-grain layer over flat color reads far more crafted.

NEAR'S IMAGERY AND ART DIRECTION: No photography of people; the hero is a full-bleed autoplaying muted looping mp4 of abstract 3D motion graphics (object-cover, absolutely positioned behind the display-l headline), supported by canvas elements running generative art on mint #C7F5D8, and CDN-served abstract renders. Partner proof is handled as a grid of 175px by 175px rounded tiles (w-[175px] h-[175px] overflow-hidden rounded) with logos absolutely centered via top-1/2 left-1/2 -translate-x-1/2 -translate-y-1/2, naming NVIDIA, Intel, Brave, Ledger, Venice AI, and the Government of Bermuda. The lesson: pick one media type per section (video hero, generative canvas, logo tiles) and keep decorative images alt="" while logos get real alt text.

NEAR'S INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE: The homepage runs as one narrative spine: H1 "The currency of agents" (a bold 4-word thesis), then "What is NEAR?", then "The NEAR Stack" broken into six h5-styled product rows (near.com, NEAR Intents, NEAR AI, NEAR Protocol, NEAR Tokenomics, NEAR House of Stake), then a full-width pull-quote as an H2, then social proof ("Integrated across enterprises, governments, wallets, and global infrastructure"), then "What's new on NEAR" content feed, then "Subscribe to NEAR news", then a footer of h5 columns (About NEAR, Tech Stack, Social). The lesson: thesis first, definition second, product stack third, proof fourth, news and capture last; every section heading is a claim, not a label.

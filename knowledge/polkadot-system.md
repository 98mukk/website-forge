# Polkadot.com Design System (live site, dissected July 2026)

POLKADOT'S COLOR SYSTEM: The entire site is a warm-grey monochrome built on Tailwind's stone scale, remapped as semantic tokens: light mode uses --color-bg #fafaf9, --color-surface #ffffff, --color-text-primary #1c1917, --color-text-secondary #57534e, --color-text-tertiary #78716c, --color-border #e7e5e4; dark mode flips to --color-bg #0f0f0f, --color-surface #1c1917, text #fafaf9 and #d6d3d1. There is exactly one chromatic color on the whole site: the brand pink rgb(255 40 103), hex #FF2867, and it appears only on keyboard focus rings (.focus:ring-accent). Even links use #57534e as their accent in light mode. The lesson: a site can be instantly recognizable with zero brand color in the layout; restraint plus one surgical accent reads as far more confident than pink everywhere, and the semantic token layer (--color-bg, --color-surface, --color-accent) is what makes the light/dark flip trivial.

POLKADOT'S TYPOGRAPHY: A three-font system declared as CSS variables: --font-sans "DM Sans" (weights 300, 400, 500, 600, 700) for body and UI, --font-serif "DM Serif Display" (400 only, normal and italic) reserved exclusively for headings, and --font-mono "JetBrains Mono" (400, 500, 600) for code. Body is set at 400 weight with font-feature-settings "liga" 1, "calt" 1 and text-rendering optimizeLegibility. The pairing rule a builder should copy: display serif for every heading, humanist sans for everything else, and never load display weights you do not use; Polkadot ships DM Serif Display in a single weight and it still carries the whole personality of the site.

POLKADOT'S TYPE SCALE: The hero h1 is font-serif at text-6xl (3.75rem) scaling to 4.5rem at md and 6rem at lg, with tracking-tight (-0.02em letter-spacing) and line-height 1. Section headings step down cleanly: page-section h2 at 2.25rem to 3rem (text-4xl md:text-5xl), card h2 at 1.875rem (text-3xl), sub-heads at 1.5rem (text-2xl) and 1.25rem, hero subline at 1.25rem to 1.875rem in font-light (300). Body copy is 1rem with a generous 1.625 line-height (leading-relaxed), lead paragraphs 1.25rem (text-xl) capped at max-w-3xl. The lesson: a roughly 1.25x modular scale with line-height collapsing to 1 at display sizes and opening to 1.625 at body sizes is what makes a serif-led page feel both monumental and readable.

POLKADOT'S SHAPE AND SPACING SYSTEM: Border radii come in a tight custom ramp of 4px, 8px (rounded), 12px (rounded-lg), 16px (rounded-xl) and 9999px pills; note they bumped Tailwind defaults up one step, so their "lg" is 12px and "xl" is 16px. The primary button is an inverted monochrome block: px-8 py-4, rounded-xl (16px), bg-grey-900 with white text in light mode and bg-grey-100 with grey-900 text in dark mode, hover shifting one stone step (grey-800 / grey-200) over 200ms. Content lives in max-w-5xl or max-w-6xl containers with px-6 md:px-12, sections padded py-20 to py-24 and separated by 1px border-t in grey-200/grey-800 rather than background color changes. The lesson: hairline borders plus one-step hover shifts keep a monochrome UI legible without shadows or color.

POLKADOT'S MOTION SYSTEM: Two layers of motion with distinct curves. Micro-interactions use CSS transitions at 0.15s to 0.2s with cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1) (Tailwind's default ease) for colors, links and toggles. Content entrances use Framer Motion with opacity 0 to 1 plus y offsets of 20 to 30px, duration 0.8s on the expo-out curve cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1), staggered with delays of 0.1s, 0.15s, 0.2s, 0.3s, 0.35s, and whileInView with viewport once:true so sections animate a single time on scroll. Ambient loops run much slower: 1.2s linear, 2s to 2.5s eases like cubic-bezier(.25,.1,.25,1) and cubic-bezier(.4,0,1,1), a 9s cycle, and the hero tagline rotates on a 10150ms interval. Everything respects prefers-reduced-motion. The lesson: snappy .15s for UI feedback, 0.8s expo-out for entrances, 10s ambience for atmosphere; never one duration for all three jobs.

POLKADOT'S SIGNATURE MOVE, THE GENERATIVE GRAYSCALE HERO: There are no photographs, no illustrations and no static gradients anywhere; the CSS contains zero gradient definitions and zero img tags, and the CSP even blocks external images (img-src 'self' data:). Instead the full-viewport hero background is a lazy-loaded Three.js fullscreen plane running a custom GLSL fragment shader called GrayscaleShader: 4-octave hash-noise fbm, two drifting "wisp" light sources, and a u_darkMode uniform that cross-fades palettes between light values vec3(0.98)/0.92/0.75/0.5 and dark values vec3(0.02)/0.08/0.25/0.5, eased at 0.05 lerp per frame, rendered at dpr capped to 1.5 for performance. On top sits the serif wordmark "Polkadot" and a shuffled rotating tagline drawn from six alliterative lines: "Products for people. Protocols for prosperity. Software for sovereignty. Instruments for individuality. Apps for agency. Crypto beyond currency." The lesson: replace stock imagery with one cheap procedural shader and strong copywriting; atmosphere plus language is the art direction.

POLKADOT'S INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE: The page reads as a manifesto, not a product grid. Order: a slim announcement bar (Web3 Summit 2026, grey-100 background, dot-separated inline text), a navbar with only Philosophy, Tech, Platform, Ecosystem, Sign in and a theme toggle, then a full-viewport hero (h1 plus rotating tagline, no CTA button in the hero), then a 3-column values grid with serif heads "Honest by design", "You own it", "Built for humans", then a long-form essay section "A new age of internet-enabled society" split into a 2-column "The Problem" / "A Different Way" argument ending in "They took the internet from us. We're taking it back.", and finally a single "Stay Updated" CTA that is just a Follow-on-X button. The lesson: when the product is a belief system, structure the homepage as thesis, values, argument, single call to action; one CTA at the end outperforms a CTA in every section for this kind of brand.

POLKADOT'S DARK-FIRST THEMING AND CRAFT DETAILS: The ThemeProvider defaults to dark ("dark" is written to localStorage on first visit) and theming is a .dark class on html swapping the semantic custom properties, with every utility written in pairs like text-grey-900 dark:text-grey-100 and bg-grey-50 dark:bg-grey-950. Accessibility is unusually complete for a marketing site: a skip-to-content link styled with the pink focus ring, aria-labels on the announcement region and nav, sr-only "(opens in new tab)" suffixes on external links, aria-hidden on the decorative shader canvas, and :focus-visible outlines in the accent color with 2px offset. The lesson a builder should steal: pick dark as the designed-first mode if that is the brand's home turf, express the theme entirely through semantic tokens so components never hardcode color, and treat focus states as the one place the brand color is guaranteed to appear.

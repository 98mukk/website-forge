# Render Network Foundation (renderfoundation.com) Design System

RENDER'S COLOR SYSTEM: A light gallery base of #fff, #f7f7f7, and #f2f2f2 carries near-black ink text tokens #012 (a subtle navy-black used on all display headlines), #000, #222, and #1c1c1c, with muted grays #888, #a8a8a8, and #aeb6c2 for labels and secondary copy. Dark event and footer sections flip to #181818, #141414, and rgb(34,34,34). Accent color is used with extreme restraint: link blue #09f (rgb(0,153,255)), plus reserved brand tokens #4181ff (blue) and #ff613e (orange) that appear as custom properties but almost never on screen. The lesson: keep the chrome nearly monochrome so the full-color 3D artwork is the only saturated thing on the page, and define accents as design tokens even if you rarely deploy them.

RENDER'S TYPE SYSTEM: One custom grotesque does everything. The stack is "Noi Grotesk Trial" in four cuts (Regular, Medium, Semibold, Bold) all declared at font-weight 400 with the weight baked into the family name, with Inter and Inter Display loaded as the UI fallback layer. Display headlines are Noi Grotesk Semibold at 56px desktop, stepping to 42px tablet and 36px mobile, with line-height exactly 1em and letter-spacing -1.8px, colored #012. Section headings run 32px, 28px, and 24px at line-height 1.2em with letter-spacing -0.02em in #000. The lesson: a single-family system with responsive fixed steps (56/42/36) and solid-leading (1em) headlines reads as engineered and premium; avoid mixing typefaces.

RENDER'S BODY AND LABEL SCALE: Body copy is Noi Grotesk Regular at 22px for lead paragraphs and 16px for standard text, both at line-height 1.3em with a distinctive -0.7px letter-spacing that tightens even the small sizes. Meta labels are 14px Semibold and 18px Bold in #888 with 0 tracking, and Fragment Mono appears for code-flavored moments. The lesson: negative tracking on body text (not just headlines) plus gray 14px caps-height labels creates the tight technical texture; keep paragraph line-height low (1.3) when line lengths are short.

RENDER'S SHAPE AND SURFACE LANGUAGE: Corner radii stay small and structural: 10px on cards and media frames (the most common value, 28+ uses), 8px on banners and buttons, 4px on small chips, never pills. Overlay UI sitting on imagery is glassy white at low alpha: rgba(255,255,255,0.15) and 0.2 for chips and badges, 0.85 for solid-feeling panels, with hairline borders like rgba(153,153,153,0.4) and whisper shadows such as rgba(0,0,0,0.05). The lesson: 4/8/10px radii plus translucent white overlays let UI float on top of full-bleed artwork without competing with it.

RENDER'S MOTION SYSTEM: Animations use custom framer-motion bezier arrays, never browser defaults: ease [0.44, 0, 0.56, 1] (a symmetric easeInOut) at 0.3s for interface state changes, [0.6, 0.2, 0, 1] (a sharp-in, long-glide-out curve) at a slow cinematic 1.5s for hero and banner reveals, and 0.4s for mid-weight transitions. The lesson: pair one fast utilitarian curve (~300ms) with one very slow signature reveal (~1500ms) so big imagery breathes while controls stay snappy.

RENDER'S GRADIENT SIGNATURE: On dark sections, text itself is a gradient: linear-gradient(337deg, rgb(255,255,255) -193%, rgba(153,153,153,0.4) 100%) clipped to the type, so headlines fade from bright white toward translucent gray at an off-axis 337 degree angle (a hero variant uses stops at -28% and 121%). A secondary metallic pill uses linear-gradient(270deg, rgb(169,182,184) 0%, rgb(101,102,108) 100%). The lesson: gradient-clipped text with stops pushed far outside the 0-100% range gives a soft cinematic sheen instead of a rainbow; keep gradients grayscale.

RENDER'S ART DIRECTION: Imagery is exclusively high-end photoreal 3D renders made by the network's own community: dark cinematic sci-fi cityscapes, purple-and-gold nebulae, moody CGI worlds served as full-bleed jpegs from a rotating pool of roughly 20 images. Every hero image carries a small glass credit chip reading "Cloud-rendered by" plus the artist handle (Mat17rix_3d, justingraphics, annibale_siconolfi, PENTZ), turning the product's output into the brand's entire visual identity. The lesson: when the product creates visuals, use real customer output as the art direction and credit the makers on-canvas; never use stock or generic illustration.

RENDER'S INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE: The homepage runs hero H1 "Advancing the Next Generation of Rendering and AI Technology" over rotating artwork, then a dark RenderCon event banner ("April 16-17, 2026", Nya Studios, Hollywood), then four pillar links styled as headline-weight items (Governance, Whitepaper, Team, Grants), then a giant mission statement set as an H1 paragraph, a live proof stat ("frames rendered since you've arrived" ticking counter), four value-prop sections (Revolution, Monetization, Efficiency, Community), news modules (New AI Tools, RNDR to RENDER Upgrade), a "Partner With Us" CTA, and a #222 footer. The lesson: order the page as proof-first storytelling: identity, live event, navigation-as-content, mission, live metric, benefits, news, single CTA.

RENDER'S SIGNATURE MOVES: The recognizable fingerprint is (1) full-bleed community 3D renders with on-image "Cloud-rendered by" glass credit chips, (2) massive solid-leading headlines (56px, line-height 1em, -1.8px tracking) in near-black #012 on white, (3) grayscale gradient-clipped text at 337deg on #181818 dark sections, and (4) a live "frames rendered since you've arrived" counter as social proof. Built on Framer with token-driven colors and framer-motion easing. The lesson for a builder: pick one live data point, one image source with provenance, and one typographic extreme, then keep every other element quiet, small-radius, and monochrome.

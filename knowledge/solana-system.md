# Solana.com Design System (RAG Knowledge Card)

SOLANA'S COLOR SYSTEM: The entire site sits on pure black (body background rgb(0,0,0), also #121212 and near-black tints #0B0A10 and #080d17 for section panels) with white #fff body text softened to rgb(208,208,220) for paragraphs. On top of that dark canvas sit exactly two signature brand accents, purple #9945FF and mint green #14F195, plus supporting neons cyan #80ecff, blue #64a8f2, and pink #f087ff/#eb54bb used only inside gradients. Muted grays #232323, #6c6a81, #ABABBA, and low-alpha whites like #ffffff14 and #ECE4FD1F handle borders and dividers. Lesson for builders: a dark-mode brand feels premium when 95 percent of the page is black and gray and the saturated accents are rationed to two hero hues that appear mostly as gradients, never as flat fills on large areas.

SOLANA'S GRADIENT LANGUAGE: Gradients are the brand's real logo. The canonical strokes are linear-gradient(225deg, #14f195 16.12%, #64a8f2 49.58%, #9945ff 83.36%) and linear-gradient(267.25deg, #9946ff 2.48%, #15f195 95.66%), a green-to-blue-to-purple sweep applied to headline text, button borders, and glowing card edges. Secondary gradient families run pink to violet, linear-gradient(100.51deg, #f087ff 27.61%, #6e1fce 62.14%, transparent), and cyan to blue, linear-gradient(100.22deg, #1fcff1 30.11%, #234cb6 66.43%, transparent), always fading to transparent at one end so they look like light beams rather than filled shapes. There are also vertical fade masks like linear-gradient(180deg, rgba(255,255,255,0)) and #121212-to-transparent overlays that melt imagery into the black background. Lesson: define two or three named multi-stop gradients with fixed angles and stop percentages and reuse them verbatim everywhere so the brand stays recognizable.

SOLANA'S TYPOGRAPHY STACK: Body copy is the custom sans Diatype at font-size 1.3125rem (21px) with line-height 140 percent and color #fff on black, with a fallback stack of Helvetica, Arial, system-ui. Big numeric stats (50M, 3.5B, $3.3T) switch to a custom brand monospace DSemi via a .font-brand-mono class (font-family: DSemi, monospace), and code uses ABC Mono or Roboto Mono; Space Grotesk and Monument exist as secondary display faces. Lesson: one workhorse grotesque for everything plus a distinctive mono reserved exclusively for numbers and data gives a fintech or crypto site instant technical credibility without font chaos.

SOLANA'S TYPE SCALE: The hero h1 uses font-size clamp(3.5rem, 8vw, 6rem) at weight 700, line-height 100 percent, letter-spacing -0.03rem, so it fluidly scales from 56px on mobile to 96px on desktop with zero breakpoint jumps. Section h2s use clamp(2.625rem, 8vw, 4.5rem) with letter-spacing -0.0425rem, and the tokenized nd-heading classes step 2xl from 40px to 56px to 88px (letter-spacing tightening from -1.2px to -2.64px), xl from 40px to 80px, and l from 32px to 64px, all at weight 500 with line-height 1.0 to 1.25. Lesson: pair every size increase with tighter negative letter-spacing and line-height near 1.0, and use clamp() with an 8vw preferred value so headlines feel massive but never overflow.

SOLANA'S SHAPE AND RADIUS SYSTEM: A base token --radius: 0.5rem drives calc(var(--radius) - 2px) and calc(var(--radius) - 4px) for nested elements, while cards and media step up through 0.75rem, 1rem, 16px, 18px, 20px, and 30px, and pills and buttons use 9999px full-round (buttons also carry --btn-border-radius: 6px). Hairline borders are 1px in low-alpha white like #ffffff14 or #ECE4FD33 rather than gray, so edges glow faintly against black. Lesson: derive inner radii from the outer container radius minus the padding delta (the minus 2px and minus 4px calc pattern) so nested rounded corners always look concentric instead of mismatched.

SOLANA'S MOTION SYSTEM: The dominant easing is cubic-bezier(.4, 0, .2, 1) (Material standard ease, used 17 times) with micro-interactions at .15s (25 uses), .2s, and .3s; slower reveals run .5s to .7s and one expressive curve cubic-bezier(.16, 1, .3, 1) handles springy entrances. The body itself has transition: background-color .3s ease, letting whole sections crossfade their background as you scroll, and ambient loops like marquees and glows run 5s to 8s. Lesson: standardize on one ease and three durations (150ms hover, 300ms state, 600ms reveal) and reserve long 8s infinite animations for decorative background motion only.

SOLANA'S IMAGERY AND ART DIRECTION: There is no photography of people in the core layout; imagery is abstract 3D-rendered light streaks and geometric decor (decor-bg.webp, pattern-parallelogram-r.svg) placed as absolutely positioned, oversized, bleeding layers with classes like absolute bottom-[-100px] right-[-1000px] mix-blend-overlay opacity-40 and blurred glow plates like opacity-30 blur-[40px]. Real photos appear only inside content cards (event covers, blog heroes) always as object-cover z-0 fills behind text, and roughly 20 partner logos (Visa, PayPal, BlackRock, Western Union, Franklin Templeton) render as white monochrome SVGs in uniform-height logo walls. Lesson: keep hero art abstract and brand-gradient-colored, push it off-canvas with negative positioning and mix-blend-overlay so it reads as atmosphere, and confine literal photography to card thumbnails.

SOLANA'S INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE: The homepage order is a single bold claim h1 (The capital market for every asset on earth), then community proof (Meet Solana IRL events grid), then performance claims (The fastest growing and leading financial platform, with live stats in DSemi mono like 3.5B and $3.3T), then real-time activity (What's happening right now), then institutional trust (The world's largest institutions and fintechs are building on Solana with the logo wall), then content (Keep up with the latest, How we built this stories), then a participation CTA section (Build alongside our global community) split into five h4 actions: Validate the network, Secure by staking, Build an app, Contribute with code, Tokenize an asset. Lesson: sequence a landing page as claim, proof, momentum, trust, content, then a segmented call to action that names each audience's specific next step.

SOLANA'S SIGNATURE MOVES: The three moves that make the site instantly recognizable are the green-to-purple 225deg gradient applied to text and glowing edges on a pure black field, giant clamp()-scaled Diatype headlines with aggressive negative tracking, and DSemi monospace stat counters presented as proof points. Everything decorative fades to transparent (gradients, image masks, section crossfades), so color always behaves like emitted light rather than paint. Lesson: to clone this energy, pick one dark canvas, one two-color gradient identity, one display face with tight tracking, and let every accent dissolve to transparency instead of ending in a hard edge.

SOLANA MOTION, HERO RIBBON ANATOMY (deep scan): The homepage hero's glossy undulating wave is a WebGL shader (Unicorn Studio scene, 30fps capped, lazy-loaded, with a static webp fallback), not video or CSS. The ribbon is a thin sheet displaced by summed sine waves, wave1 = sin(x*10 + t*0.05)*0.5 plus wave2 = cos((x+0.92)*7.5 + t*0.075)*0.5, with time coefficients of 0.05 to 0.075, meaning the undulation is VERY slow, barely perceptible. The iridescent purple-green sheen comes from per-channel chromatic refraction (coefficients 0.03/0.06/0.1 so blue bends most), a lavender #B69AFF fresnel rim highlight, and tight specular light from upper-left, layered under fbm noise and film grain. Lesson: premium ambient motion is SLOW summed sine waves plus one sharp lavender highlight edge on near-black, never fast or looping-obvious.

SOLANA MOTION, FAKING THE RIBBON WITHOUT WEBGL: In a single-file HTML site, approximate the hero ribbon with a small inline 2D canvas at ~30fps: draw 3 to 5 horizontal ribbon paths using y = sin(x*k + t*0.05)*A + cos((x+0.92)*k*0.75 + t*0.075)*A, stroke each with a linear gradient #9945FF to #5497D5 to #14F195 at low alpha, duplicate strokes offset ±2px in red/blue-shifted tints to fake chromatic dispersion, and soften with CSS filter blur(2px) plus a faint noise overlay. Pure-CSS fallback: 2 or 3 absolutely positioned skewed gradient bands, linear-gradient(105deg, transparent, #9945FF33, #14F19533, transparent), with filter blur(40px), mix-blend-mode screen, animated by 20 to 40 second ease-in-out infinite alternate keyframes moving translate3d and rotate a few percent. Always pause under prefers-reduced-motion.

SOLANA MOTION, REVEAL AND HOVER VOCABULARY (deep scan): Content reveals are small and fast, never over 0.8s: fadeInUp (opacity 0 + translateY(50px) to 0) at .6s ease-out forwards, fadeInRight .6s ease-out, and clip-path curtains from inset(0 100% 0 0) to inset(0) at .6s to .8s ease-out. Hovers are overwhelmingly transition-colors at 200ms with the Material curve cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1); one expressive curve cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1) at .7s handles springy hero entrances. Signature hover: the shimmer border, a conic-gradient(from var(--rotation), #9945FF, #14F195 60%, #00D4FF 80%, #9945FF) painted behind a padding-box fill, rotating 360deg over 6s linear infinite only while hovered. Lesson: content moves fast (200 to 800ms), only ambient decoration is allowed slow infinite loops, and cap the page at ONE expensive animated background with everything else as cheap color transitions.

SOLANA HEADER/NAV BEHAVIOR: The header is sticky top-0 z-50 wrapping a nav with py-3, transition-colors duration-300, a lavender-tinted hairline border rgba(240,228,255,0.12), and a permanently 95-percent-opaque background rgb(18 18 18/95%) — no shrink-on-scroll, just a fixed bar whose color can cross-fade in 300ms. Inner row: max-w-[1440px] px-[20px] xl:px-[14px] mx-auto flex justify-between. Desktop nav appears only at xl (1280px, hamburger persists through tablet); each nav trigger is a text pill h-9 px-3.5 text-[15px] at 64-percent-white, rounded-full, hover:text-white hover:bg-white/[0.08] with 200ms color transition, chevron rotating 180deg when open. Lesson: a sticky bar with one fixed 95-percent-alpha background plus tinted hairline is simpler and steadier than scroll-triggered blur or shrink logic.

SOLANA MEGA-MENU AND MOBILE MENU: Dropdown panels are absolute top-[45px], min-w-[320px], bg rgba(25,24,27,0.92) with backdrop-blur(20px), rounded-2xl, border white/[0.06], shadow 0 20px 60px -12px rgba(0,0,0,0.6), and max-h calc(100dvh - 5rem) with internal overscroll-contained scrolling. Mobile menu is a sheet sliding from the top (slide-in-from-top, 300ms) over a fixed black/80 overlay, with swipe-to-close at a 50px threshold; the hamburger's three bars morph to an X via rotate-45/translate-y-[6px] at 300ms; mobile items become full-width rows split by border-b white/10. Sitewide z-index vocabulary stays tiny: -1/0/1/2/3, 10, 40, 50. Lesson: build menus on one z-50 layer with 92-percent-alpha blurred panels and dvh-clamped internal scroll, and keep the whole site's z-index scale under about 5 values.

SOLANA BUTTONS: Primary CTA is a white pill with a nested black circle: outer h-11 to h-[48px] rounded-full px-5 bg-white text-black tracking -0.16px, hover bg-white/90; inner span -mr-3 size-8 bg-black text-white rounded-full holding a 16px arrow icon, the negative margin tucking the circle against the pill edge. The floating AI button is the fanciest build: fixed bottom-8 right-8, rounded-full, bg gradient-to-b from black to #14001D, backdrop-blur(3px), with a three-part inset shadow stack — 0 -4px 12px 0 #361B40 inset (inner purple glow), 0 1px 0 0 #C37CE0 inset (top bevel), 0 -1px 0 0 #D06BF8 inset (bottom rim light) — whose hover swaps all three to brighter hexes. Focus is focus-visible:ring-2 with ring-offset-2. Lesson: premium buttons come from stacked inset shadows (glow plus bevel plus rim) and a pill-in-pill icon chip, not from bigger drop shadows.

SOLANA ANIMATED CONIC BORDER CARDS: The signature card border is CSS-only: register @property --rotation as an angle, then border 1px solid transparent, border-radius 16px, background linear-gradient(#121212,#121212) padding-box stacked over conic-gradient(from var(--rotation), metallic whites and blues) border-box, inset box-shadow 0 0 0 .5px white/.06, and an 8s linear infinite keyframe that just rotates --rotation 0 to 360deg; the brand variant runs 6s with #9945ff, #14f195 60%, #00d4ff 80%, #9945ff. Glass cards use backdrop-filter blur(12px) over black at 50-percent alpha, radius .5rem, padding 1.5rem. Image cards are relative rounded-xl overflow-hidden group with group-hover:scale-110 on the img, the only hover-transform on the page. Lesson: animate a registered @property angle inside a padding-box/border-box double background to get a rotating gradient border with zero JavaScript and zero extra DOM.

SOLANA SPACING AND CONTAINERS: Sections are full-bleed relative overflow-hidden bg-black with one inner wrapper max-w-screen-2xl (1440px) mx-auto px-5 md:px-8 xl:px-10 py-10 — a flat 40px vertical padding rhythm with height coming from content and min-height (hero: min-h-[700px] plus min-h calc(100vh - 70px) and xl:pt-[165px]). Grid gaps commonly md:gap-12 xl:gap-16 up to gap-20; feature grids run grid-cols-2 xl:grid-cols-4 with column count chosen from item count (2 items 2 cols, 3 items 3, more 4), and cells separated by conditional hairline borders computed from index instead of gaps. Lesson: pick one constant section wrapper (1440px, 20/32/40px side padding, about 40px vertical padding) and let min-height plus content set the rhythm.

SOLANA SCROLL CHOREOGRAPHY: Reveals are IntersectionObserver-driven at threshold 0.2 with trigger-once latching, toggling animate-fade-in-up (fadeInUp .6s ease-out forwards from opacity 0 translateY(50px), initial state opacity 0), staggered via inline animationDelay 0.1 + 0.1*i seconds (one section reverse-staggers 1 + (n-i)*0.15s). Media reveals use clip-path curtains: inset(0 100% 0 0) to inset(0) over .8s ease-out, or curtain-top-to-bottom pairing the clip with transform scale(1.1) to 1 over .6s. Lazy content mounts with rootMargin 200px; prefers-reduced-motion rules ship in the bundles. Lesson: one fadeInUp keyframe plus a threshold-0.2 observer plus index-times-100ms inline delays covers 90 percent of scroll choreography; save clip-path curtains for imagery.

SOLANA RESPONSIVE STRATEGY: Breakpoints are Tailwind defaults plus 1440, but the structural switch is xl (1280px), not 768: full nav, expanded search box with a command-K kbd chip, and the larger logo appear only at 1280 and up, while below it search collapses to an icon and the sheet menu takes over. Component swaps per breakpoint: nav text 16 to 15px, buttons 36 to 44px tall, tracking tightening -0.14 to -0.16px; mobile carousels use native scroll-snap (snap-x snap-mandatory, children snap-start); decorative blend-mode imagery is desktop-only via hidden xl:block. Lesson: collapse to the mobile pattern at 1280px, not 768 — a hamburger on tablet beats a cramped nav — and tune px-level type, height, and tracking per breakpoint.

SOLANA DETAIL POLISH: ::selection is brand green #00ffbd with near-black text via themable vars. Scrollbars: webkit width 5px, thumb rgba(115,115,115,.5) radius 4px with hover .7 and active .9 states, transparent track. Hairlines are lavender-tinted tokens #ECE4FD1F and #ECE4FD33 rather than plain white alpha, warming every edge. Decorative texture comes from off-canvas SVG and webp strips composited with mix-blend-overlay at opacity 40 pushed right-[-900px], and mix-blend-plus-lighter light streaks pinned to section edges. Dark is the DEFAULT stylesheet and a .light ancestor class activates overrides. Modern CSS in production: text-wrap balance and pretty, 100dvh, overscroll-contain, @property. Lesson: tint hairlines toward the brand hue, brand the text selection color, and treat light mode as the override when the product is dark-first.

SOLANA FONT AND PERF STRATEGY: Fonts are self-hosted (Diatype weights 200-700 woff2, all font-display swap) with no third-party font hosts and no font preloads; instead the LCP hero background webp is preloaded via link rel=preload as=image with imagesrcset up to 3840w at quality 75. 47 of 50 images are loading=lazy; the hero background is an eager absolute inset-0 object-cover fill. Favicon ships as an inline base64 SVG data URI (zero-request vector) plus PNG and apple-touch fallbacks. GPU hints via transform-gpu on large rounded media bands. Lesson: preload the LCP background image, not the fonts; swap-display self-hosted fonts; inline the SVG favicon as a data URI.

SOLANA ACCESSIBILITY AND COMPONENT HYGIENE: Sections are labelled regions (section id=hero aria-labelledby=hero-title with the h1 carrying that id); the h1 splits visual weight inside one heading (first line regular, second line font-light span). Menus expose aria-expanded, aria-controls, and data-state; the mobile sheet includes a visually-hidden title for screen readers; icon links get aria-label plus generous p-2.5 hit areas; decorative SVGs are aria-hidden and decorative imgs use empty alt. Disabled states are pointer-events-none opacity-50; svg children inside buttons are neutralized with pointer-events-none and fixed size-4. Lesson: lean on headless primitives for state and ARIA wiring and spend custom code purely on the visual layer.

SOLANA COMMUNITY SECTION, BACKGROUND ART ASSETS: The nebula is a single flat image, not layered art: projects-bg.webp, a 1360x720 WebP weighing only ~12KB (heavy blur compresses beautifully), served through Next.js image optimization at up to w=3840 q=75 with sizes 150vw. That WebP is only the static fallback: after load a WebGL scene (30fps) replaces it in the same slot with 4 layers: solid-black gradient base, an AI-GENERATED image layer (a Midjourney PNG whose filename literally contains the prompt "dark black background with purple and green gradient"), an animated fbm noise effect (speed 0.33, mouseMomentum 0.07) that makes the aurora drift, and a blur pass. There is NO wireframe-grid asset; the grid impression comes from the translucent card lattice sitting on top. Lesson: ship a tiny blurred WebP as the instant static layer, upgrade to an animated shader scene after load, and let AI-generated gradient art plus procedural noise replace expensive video backgrounds.

SOLANA COMMUNITY SECTION, LAYERING RECIPE: The section is relative overflow-hidden on pure black with an inner frame max-w-[1828px] mx-auto rounded-xl overflow-hidden transform-gpu, so the art clips into a floating rounded card with 8px black gutters. The background img uses fill mode (absolute inset-0 z-0, object-fit cover; the animated scene mounts at height 101% to hide edge seams). The readability trick is ONE overlay div: absolute top, height 80%, bg-gradient-to-b from #0B0A10 via #0B0A10 at 19% to transparent, pointer-events-none, so the headline sits on clean black while the glow only blooms behind the bottom rows. No blend modes, no z-index games: z-0 art, one scrim, relative content. Lesson: one absolutely-positioned cover image at z-0, one top-down gradient scrim in the page background color, and relative content is the entire recipe for text-over-glow sections.

SOLANA COMMUNITY SECTION, GLASS ACTION ROWS: The five actions are plain anchor tags in a grid (1 col mobile, 2 tablet, 3 desktop) with gap-1, so the "borders" are just 4px gutters of dark background. Each card: rounded-xl, background #ECE4FD1F (lavender-white at ~12% alpha), backdrop-blur 8px frosting the nebula behind it, hover brightens to white/20; the whole card is the link, no button, no border, no shadow, no JavaScript. Icons are inline 40x40 stroke-currentColor SVGs; mobile flips to horizontal (icon left) while desktop stacks icon above text. The section header pairs a bold h2 with a font-light span, and the subtitle carries a 1px vertical gradient rule pseudo-element from #D884F0 (purple) to #44EBA6 (green), echoing the nebula hues. Lesson: translucent white-at-12 fills + backdrop blur + whole-card anchors with a brighter hover fill gives premium glass rows with zero borders and zero JS.

SOLANA NEXT-IMAGE PIPELINE: The /_next/image?url=...&w=3840&q=75 URLs in DevTools are Next.js's built-in optimizer endpoint, not real files: url= is the URL-encoded source path, w= a width bucket, q= quality. The server resizes/re-encodes to WebP/AVIF per the browser's Accept header, caches at the CDN, and never upscales (a 1360px source requested at w=3840 just returns the original). Lesson: commit one master asset and let the framework serve responsive modern formats; decode the url= parameter in DevTools to find the true source file every time.

# Stellar / SDF Press + Logo Kit (official PDF scan)

STELLAR BRAND KIT, OFFICIAL LOGO ASSETS (LOGO PRESS KIT 2026): Official logos ship in exactly two brand entities: "Stellar" (network wordmark) and "Stellar Development Foundation" (SDF lockup: circular starburst rune symbol + three-line "Stellar Development Foundation" wordmark). Each entity comes in only two colorways, Final Black and Final White, delivered in RGB (PNG, SVG, AI, PDF) and CMYK (AI, EPS, PDF) folders, plus press thumbnails in 1:1, 2:1, 3:2, 4:3, 5:4, and 16:9 aspect ratios. There are NO color, gradient, or outlined logo variants in the official kit. Lesson: on the web, use the white or black logo SVG only and let the background carry the color; never tint, recolor, or gradient the mark itself.

STELLAR BRAND KIT, LOGO USAGE LAW: Because the 2026 kit provides only black-on-light and white-on-dark versions, the implicit prohibition set is: no recolored marks, no low-contrast placements, no redrawn symbols, and no mixing the Stellar wordmark with the SDF lockup as if interchangeable. SDF's press kit explicitly directs users to download logo variants from the official kit and "refer to our brand policy for guidelines on usage" (stellar.org brand policy governs all third-party use). Lesson: pull logo files straight from the official kit folders rather than screenshotting or rebuilding them, and link brand-policy compliance into any client deliverable.

STELLAR BRAND KIT, VISUAL SIGNATURE (PRESS KIT COVER): The 2024 press kit cover sets the black three-line SDF logo lockup inside a rough, hand-painted YELLOW BRUSHSTROKE swatch on a white page: the signature "yellow paint smear" device from the 2023 rebrand (unveiled alongside the Idris Elba collaboration). Headers are set in a heavy, condensed, all-caps display face ("PRESS KIT", "EXECUTIVE BIOS", "STELLAR TIMELINE"), while body copy is a clean grotesque sans in sentence case. Note: the press kit PDF does not publish exact hex codes or licensed font names; those must come from the live-site scan (stellar-system.md) or stellar.org/brand-policy. Lesson: pair one loud condensed all-caps display header with quiet grotesque body text, and use the yellow brush texture as an accent container, not a background wash.

STELLAR BRAND KIT, NAMING AND ENTITY RULES: Three distinct names, never conflated: "Stellar" = the whole (more than a blockchain: network + ecosystem + foundation); "the Stellar network" = the decentralized, fast, scalable, uniquely sustainable blockchain built for financial products and services; "Stellar Development Foundation (SDF)" = the non-profit (founded 2014) that supports the network's development. Standard boilerplate: SDF is "a non-profit organization focused on working with and supporting changemakers to create equitable access to the global financial system through blockchain technology." Lesson: in site copy, always write "the Stellar network" for the tech and "SDF" for the org; calling the foundation "Stellar" is an official-usage error.

STELLAR BRAND KIT, BRAND VOICE AND POSITIONING: Key approved phrases: "Stellar is more than a blockchain"; "where blockchain meets the real world"; "unlock the world's economic potential through blockchain technology"; the network is "decentralized, fast, scalable, and uniquely sustainable," "built with speed and low costs in mind," making "money more fluid, markets more open, and people more empowered." Tone is mission-driven, real-world-utility focused, and equity-oriented; never speculative or crypto-hype. Lesson: write Stellar-flavored copy around financial access and real-world utility, and avoid price/trading/hype language entirely.

STELLAR BRAND KIT, OFFICIAL FACTS AND PROOF POINTS: Founded 2014 (network + SDF); Stellar Consensus Protocol adopted 2015; rebrand + Idris Elba collaboration unveiled 2023; $100M Soroban Adoption Fund 2023; smart contracts live on Stellar 2024; by 2024 the network surpassed 8 million accounts and 15 billion operations; partners include MoneyGram (global cash-out), Franklin Templeton (money market fund on-chain), and UNHCR (Stellar Aid Assist in Ukraine). Leadership for bios/quotes: Denelle Dixon (CEO, ex-Mozilla COO), Jed McCaleb (Co-Founder/Chief Architect), David Mazieres (Co-Founder/Chief Scientist, Stanford). Lesson: cite these exact figures and partners as trust signals instead of inventing stats.

STELLAR BRAND KIT, PRESS AND CONTACT CONVENTIONS: Official channels: Twitter/X @stellarorg, LinkedIn "Stellar Development Foundation", media contact media@stellar.org, foundation info at stellar.org/foundation. The press kit structure itself is a repeatable template: Executive Bios, then Network and Company Overviews (three-tier boilerplate: Stellar / The Stellar Network / SDF), then Social, Media Contact, Logos and Brand Policy, then a year-by-year Timeline. Lesson: a press/brand page needs exactly these blocks (bios, tiered boilerplate, contacts, logo downloads, timeline) so reuse this skeleton for client press kits.

STELLAR BRAND KIT, WHAT THE OFFICIAL KIT DOES NOT PROVIDE: The 2024 press kit and 2026 logo kit contain no hex palette, no clearspace/minimum-size diagrams, no typeface licensing names, and no photography rules; Stellar publishes those on its web brand-policy page, and the live-site scan (stellar-system.md) remains the source for implementation values. Lesson: official kits define the marks, names, and voice; always merge them with a live-site scan to get the buildable tokens, and flag any value that has no official source.

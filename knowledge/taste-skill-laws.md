# Anti-slop laws (distilled from the taste-skill) — the quality constitution

TASTE LAW, READ THE BRIEF FIRST: before designing, infer what the client actually wants: page kind (landing, portfolio, editorial), audience (B2B buyer, consumer, recruiter), vibe words, references. The audience picks the aesthetic, not the designer's default. State the design read in one line, then build to it.

TASTE LAW, THE THREE DIALS: set three dials per project before designing: design variance (symmetry vs asymmetric chaos), motion intensity (static vs cinematic), visual density (airy gallery vs packed cockpit). Trust-first or public-sector briefs force low variance and low motion; agency and creative briefs allow high. Declare the dials, then every layout and animation decision obeys them.

TASTE LAW, BANNED AI-SLOP DEFAULTS: purple gradients on dark, centered hero over a mesh glow, three equal feature cards, Inter/Roboto as default fonts, glassmorphism on everything, infinite looping micro-animations, gradient text on heroes, pure #000000 backgrounds, neon outer glows, custom cursors. These are the patterns every AI produces; reaching past them is the whole job.

TASTE LAW, HERO DISCIPLINE: the hero must fit the first viewport: headline max 2 lines, subtext max 20 words, CTA visible without scrolling. Maximum 4 text elements in a hero (eyebrow OR brand strip, headline, subtext, CTAs). No trust logos, pricing teasers, or feature lists inside the hero; those get their own sections below.

TASTE LAW, EYEBROW RATION: small uppercase labels above section headings (eyebrows) are allowed at most 1 per 3 sections. No section-numbering labels (01/Capabilities), no version tags (BETA, v2.0) in heroes, no "scroll to explore" cues, no decorative status dots.

TASTE LAW, LAYOUT VARIETY: no layout family repeats twice in a row; a page of 8 sections needs at least 4 distinct families (split, bento, full-width, stacked, marquee). Max 2 consecutive image+text zigzag sections. Bento grids have exactly as many cells as content items and at least a third of cells carry real visual variation (image, tint, pattern), never all white text cards.

TASTE LAW, EM-DASH TOTAL BAN: zero em-dashes anywhere on a page (headlines, body, quotes, captions, buttons). It is the number one AI writing tell. Use periods, commas, colons, or parentheses instead.

TASTE LAW, COPY REGISTER: concrete verbs over filler (banned: Elevate, Seamless, Unleash, Revolutionize, Next-Gen). Realistic names and organic numbers (47.2 percent, not 50 percent). No fake-precise specs the brand never claimed. One copy register per page. Quotes max 3 lines with full name + role attribution.

TASTE LAW, REAL IMAGES ALWAYS: a text-only page is not minimalism, it is unfinished. Every site needs at least 3 real images (generated to match the art direction, or seeded placeholders like picsum with descriptive seeds). Div-built fake screenshots and hand-drawn SVG illustrations are banned. If the imagery is the brand (illustration worlds, community art), capture and follow its art direction rules.

TASTE LAW, STATES AND MOTION: every interactive component ships hover, focus, active, disabled, loading, and empty states; loading uses skeletons shaped like the final layout, never generic spinners. Animate only transform and opacity, sub-400ms, spring or named cubic-bezier curves (browser default "ease" is lazy), nothing loops forever, and everything collapses to static under prefers-reduced-motion.

TASTE LAW, ACCESSIBILITY FLOORS: body text contrast at least WCAG AA 4.5:1, buttons readable against their fill (no white-on-white), focus rings visible and high-contrast, 44px touch targets, color never the only signal, real semantic HTML with logical heading order. These override aesthetics every time.

TASTE LAW, ONE SYSTEM PER PAGE: one theme (light or dark, never flipping mid-scroll), one accent color used identically across all sections, one corner-radius scale, one icon family, one font pairing. Consistency is what reads as professional; novelty per-section is what reads as AI.

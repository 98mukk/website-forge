# TRON Brand Guidelines (official PDF scan)

TRON BRAND KIT, BRAND POSITIONING: TRON, created in 2017, positions itself as one of the largest open blockchain platforms in the world, with the tagline "DECENTRALIZE THE WEB" used across apparel, helmets, and merch. The 2023-era rebrand was a brand upgrade manual made to "maximize retention of the brand DNA" while modernizing recognizability, ease of use, and design sense. Lesson: anchor a redesign to one preserved DNA element (here, the red diamond icon) so the brand stays recognizable while everything else modernizes.

TRON BRAND KIT, PRIMARY COLOR: The core brand color is RED: Pantone 185C, HEX #dc062b, RGB 220/6/43, CMYK 7/100/83/0. Official guidance: "Red is the core color of TRON. Red are preferred for most scenarios and sufficiently communicates our brand value"; the palette exists to bridge marketing communications and product interface for familiarity and visual recognition. Lesson: use #dc062b (not a generic crypto red) as the single dominant hue and let it carry most surfaces, not just accents.

TRON BRAND KIT, SECONDARY COLORS: Exactly three secondaries: WHITE #ffffff (RGB 255/255/255), GREY #a0a1a2 (RGB 160/161/162, CMYK 43/34/32/0), and BLACK #242729 (Pantone 426C, RGB 36/39/41, CMYK 43/77.5/73.5/54). Note the "black" is officially a soft charcoal (#242729), not pure #000000. Lesson: build dark sections on #242729 charcoal rather than pure black to match official TRON surfaces.

TRON BRAND KIT, ICON CONSTRUCTION: The diamond icon is geometrically constructed in an 11-step sequence from an inverted triangle with 60-degree top corners, a 0.6:1 internal division ratio, a counter-clockwise rotation at the end, and a fixed line weight; the manual says "please refer to the step by step diagram to draw the graphic, please avoid error and any modification." The icon is the core recognition element and "can not be used as an auxiliary graphics" (no watermarks, patterns, or decorative fills from the icon). Lesson: treat the icon as a locked SVG asset; never redraw, rotate, or repurpose it as background decoration.

TRON BRAND KIT, LOGO SYSTEM: The logo = icon + custom TRON wordmark (techy geometric letterforms: double-stroke T, circled-dot O, notched N); the two "should always try to exist together" and may never be modified, distorted, or redrawn. The wordmark alone is permitted only where the full logo is not appropriate. Lesson: default to the full lockup (icon left, wordmark right) and fall back to wordmark-only only in tight UI spots like navbars or favicons paired separately.

TRON BRAND KIT, CLEARSPACE LAW: Clearspace unit X = the diameter of the big circle in the wordmark's "O". Horizontal lockup: X clearspace on all sides, with icon-to-wordmark gap = 0.385x. Vertical lockup: X on all sides, icon-to-wordmark vertical gap = 0.563x. Partner/sponsor lockups: TRON logo and partner logo must be separated by a 2x white stroke/gap (x = the O circle). Lesson: derive logo padding from the O-circle diameter (roughly the wordmark cap-height circle), not arbitrary pixel values.

TRON BRAND KIT, LOGO COLOR HIERARCHY: Five sanctioned uses in priority order: (1) red icon + black wordmark on white (preferred), (2) all-white logo on red, (3) white logo on red alt, (4) white logo on grey (only when 1-3 fail), (5) white logo on black (last resort). Hard rule: "Never use the logo in any of the secondary colors"; no grey or off-brand tinted logos. Lesson: on light pages render the two-tone red/black logo; on any dark or red surface switch to the pure white version, nothing in between.

TRON BRAND KIT, LOGO PROHIBITIONS: The manual calls the logo "our most sacred asset" and bans eight treatments: don't rotate/turn it around, don't add gradients, don't stretch it, don't convert it to an outline stroke, don't move/reorder the icon (icon never goes right of the wordmark), don't fill the icon solid, don't use multiple colors, don't add strokes/outlines around it. Lesson: your CSS must never transform, gradient-fill, outline, or recolor the logo; scale it proportionally and leave it alone.

TRON BRAND KIT, BRAND-IN-USE STYLE: Official applications show flat, saturated full-bleed red heroes (tron.network mockup: solid #dc062b header with white logo and white nav text), white-on-red typography in a heavy extended grotesque set in all-caps, black-and-red environments with glassy 3D red crystal renders, and a playful "scattered letters" T-R-O-N motif on merch. Layouts are minimal: huge type, generous space, almost no ornament. Lesson: go bold and flat; full-bleed #dc062b sections, all-caps extended-bold white headlines, and restraint everywhere else reads instantly as TRON.

TRON BRAND KIT, VOICE AND GOVERNANCE: Copy voice is declarative, short, all-caps, and confident ("DECENTRALIZE THE WEB", "THANKS FOR WATCHING"); guidelines are bilingual English/Chinese, and all assets are governed by the TRON DESIGN CENTRE (its own stacked triangle mark appears on the cover and closing slides). Lesson: write hero copy as short imperative all-caps statements and credit/source official assets from TRON Design Centre rather than fan redraws.

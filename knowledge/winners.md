# Forge Winners: what the scoring builds did right


FORGE WINNER, PALETTE: Anchor the dark-premium fintech feel with a four-tier system that respects the 60/30/9/1 split: base #0B0F14 for the page body and deepest sections, surface #141B24 for cells, metrics band, and the sticky process card, accent #3DDC97 held to roughly 9 percent as the only saturated color, and signal #E8EDF2 for primary text. The trick that made it read premium rather than flat: derive supporting values from the accent rather than gray, using --line rgba(61,220,151,0.14) and --line-soft rgba(61,220,151,0.08) for every border so the teal quietly tints the whole structure, plus a --muted #7C8896 for secondary copy and dead #2C3642 to #4C5866 for marquee and logo text that should recede. The accent never fills a large area; it lives in 10px dots, 6px list bullets, 2px quote rules, numeric emphasis, and one solid button, which is exactly why the 9 percent ceiling holds and the base stays dominant.

FORGE WINNER, TYPE: Pair Sora for display and IBM Plex Sans for body, and let the tension between geometric headings and humanist text carry the fintech register. Headings run font-weight 600 (hero pushes 700) with letter-spacing -0.03em and a tight line-height of 1.05, so large type feels engineered and dense; body sits at 16px, line-height 1.5, letter-spacing -0.2px for calm readability. Fluid scale via clamp keeps hierarchy intact across breakpoints: hero clamp(38px,5.4vw,66px), section h2 clamp(28px,3.8vw,44px), metric bigs clamp(38px,5vw,56px). The signature move is the eyebrow: IBM Plex 12px, weight 600, letter-spacing 0.22em, uppercase, in accent teal, placed above every h2 to label the section like a system readout. Use text-wrap balance on headings and pretty on paragraphs so no line ends orphaned.

FORGE WINNER, LAYOUT: Contain everything in a 1180px wrap with 32px gutters and breathe with a vertical rhythm of 104px section padding that collapses to 72px on mobile. Break the monotony of stacked centered blocks by rotating layout archetypes down the page: a 1.05fr/0.95fr hero split, a 3-column 220px-row bento with deliberate span2 and rows2 cells for asymmetry, a full-bleed 4-up metrics band on its own surface, then alternating 1fr/1fr rows that flip order via nth-child(even), and a 0.9fr/1.1fr process split with a sticky top:100px side card. This variety is why an 8/10 reads as designed rather than templated; each section proves a different composition while the shared 10px radius, teal hairlines, and surface fills keep it one system. Every grid has an explicit single-column mobile fallback so nothing relies on shrink.

FORGE WINNER, MOTION: Keep motion purposeful and fast, never decorative-slow. Define three named easings up front (--e-hover cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1), --e-decel, --e-spring .34,1.56,.64,1) and reuse them so timing feels consistent. Scroll reveals use a single .reveal class going from opacity 0 and translateY(40px) to rest over .6s ease-out, driven by IntersectionObserver at threshold 0.2 that unobserves after firing, with a staggered transition-delay of (i%6*100)ms so groups cascade instead of popping at once. Hover feedback is tactile but restrained: buttons lift translateY(-2px) with a spring and a teal-tinted shadow 0 10px 30px rgba(61,220,151,.25), cells lift 3px and brighten their border, images scale 1.04. Ambient life comes from a 26s drifting radial glow behind the hero and a 40s linear marquee. Critically, a full prefers-reduced-motion block disables reveals, marquee, glow, and hover transforms, which is the accessibility discipline graders reward.

FORGE WINNER, COPY: Write short declarative lines that name the actual work and never trip the banned list. The hero states "We build the software small businesses actually run on" rather than any elevate-seamless filler, and every section head is a plain claim: "Two disciplines, one team that ships," "How a build actually goes." Trust is built with organic, specific numbers instead of fake-round marketing: 47.2 percent drop in manual entry, 3.1x faster quotes, 62 businesses since 2021, 91 percent renewal, 18 days to first release, budgets between 6.5k and 22k. Concreteness sells the premium positioning: name real deliverables (quote generators, intake bots, internal search, CRM wiring), show a named-attributed testimonial from Northline Freight, and locate the studio in Duluth, Minnesota with "Built by hand." The voice stays operator-to-operator, promising fixed price and shipped weeks, which matches a small-business audience far better than agency abstraction.


FORGE WINNER, PALETTE: The dark-bold order was delivered with a strict four-token ladder: base #0C0D10 at 60% carries the page background, surface #16181E at 30% lifts the marquee, split section, bento cells, and mobile menu, accent #C6F04A at 9% is rationed to eyebrows, metric numbers, hover borders, the dot, and one primary button, and signal #FF5533 at 1% is spent almost nowhere except the case-study category label. The discipline that made this read as intentional rather than muddy was never using accent for body text and never using signal twice, so the lime always feels like a real light source and the orange feels like an alarm you notice. Supporting neutrals were added outside the kit but kept quiet: --ink #EDEEF0 for primary text and --muted #8A8D96 for secondary, giving a legible three-step contrast on the near-black without ever going pure white. Hairlines are derived from the accent at low alpha (rgba(198,240,74,.14) and .08), which ties borders to the brand instead of introducing a gray line color, a trick that keeps the whole surface feeling lit by the same lime.

FORGE WINNER, TYPE: Two families do all the work with no third voice sneaking in. Space Grotesk covers every display role (h1 through h4, brand mark, eyebrows, buttons, metric numbers, the step keys) at weights 600 to 700 with tight tracking of -0.02em on headings and wide 0.22em uppercase tracking on the 12px eyebrows, so the same face reads as both loud and technical. Spline Sans handles body at 17px base with 1.6 line height, giving the paragraphs breathing room against the dark. The heading scale leans on clamp() for fluidity (hero at clamp(2.6rem,6vw,4.6rem), section h2 at clamp(2rem,4.4vw,3.1rem), closer at clamp(2.2rem,5.5vw,4rem)) so the type stays bold on mobile without a separate breakpoint. The move that earned trust: measures are capped with ch units (hero p at 34ch, closer lead at 44ch, closer h2 at 16ch) so line length stays readable regardless of viewport, and the lime em spans inside headings mark the one word that matters instead of coloring whole phrases.

FORGE WINNER, LAYOUT: A 1180px max wrap with 32px gutters anchors an asymmetric rhythm that never feels like a template. The hero splits 1.05fr / 0.95fr so text edges out image, then a full-bleed surface marquee breaks the grid before a three-column bento with 200px auto-rows and span utilities (c-tall spans two rows, c-wide spans two columns) creates visual weight without new components. Section vertical rhythm is a steady 120px top and bottom, dropping to 80px under 768px, and internal spacing uses a consistent scale (16px gap in bento and glass cards, 56px to 64px between split columns, 26px and 34px stacks in the hero copy). Alternating case rows use nth-child(even) to flip .case-media order, so two rows read as a designed sequence from one rule. The whole thing collapses cleanly: bento goes to two columns at 900px then one at 768px, splits and cases stack, and the even-row order reset prevents the image landing below its text on mobile.

FORGE WINNER, MOTION: Restraint is the signature. Color state changes fire near-instant at .02s linear (link hover, border-color, button background) so hovers feel crisp and mechanical, while spatial changes use .3s with cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1) for lifts (translateY(-2px) on buttons, -4px on cells, scale(1.04) on case images). Scroll reveals travel 40px up over .6s ease-out via IntersectionObserver at 0.2 threshold, and the clever detail is a computed stagger: each reveal's transitionDelay is set from its index among reveal siblings (idx*100ms), so groups cascade instead of popping together, and observed elements are unobserved after firing so nothing re-triggers. The metrics marquee scrolls a duplicated track over 40s linear infinite and pauses on hover, giving motion that reads as alive but never distracts. Critically, a prefers-reduced-motion block kills the marquee, forces reveals visible, and strips transitions globally, so the bold motion is fully optional.

FORGE WINNER, COPY: Every line obeys the voice tokens, and that consistency is what pushed the score up. Statements are short and verb-first ("Name the decision", "Ship the smallest test", "Trace the result", "See how I work"), the positioning line "Growth you can trace back to a decision" is echoed in the hero and footer for coherence, and none of the banned words (Elevate, Seamless, Unleash, Revolutionize, Next-Gen) appear. Numbers are concrete and carry a decimal instead of tidy fives (+31.4% MQLs, −18.6% cost per lead, 2.7x ROAS, 41.2% open rate, 12.4% recovered carts, 22.8% signup lift, 14,200 contacts, 6.3s faster loads), which reads as measured evidence rather than marketing puff. One register holds across the whole page: dry, confident, decision-focused, matching the "method, not a mood board" thesis. The closer converts that tone into an offer ("Tell me the decision you're stuck on. I'll tell you how I'd test it.") so the personality and the call to action are the same sentence.


FORGE WINNER, PALETTE: Deploy the 60/30/9/1 split exactly as tokenized. Base #F4F1EA fills 60% as page background and light sections, surface #1C1B18 takes 30% as the footer, marquee, bento cells, and closer ink block, accent #C8503A holds 9% on the primary button, eyebrows, brand ampersand, and one stat number, and signal #2F6D5B stays at a true 1% used only on the tick-list squares and a single bento cell. The discipline that made this land: derive borders from the accent at low alpha (rgba(200,80,58,0.18) for lines, 0.10 for softer) rather than introducing a neutral gray, so every hairline belongs to the same family. Muted text is a single warm gray #6b675f that reads as a dimmed base, never a cold slate. The accent never touches body copy; it is reserved for signal and action, which keeps the 9% from creeping upward and cheapening the mark.

FORGE WINNER, TYPE: Pair Fraunces at 600 for all display (h1 through h3, brand, stat numbers, marquee, hero tag) against Work Sans 400 for body, 500 for buttons and nav-hover states, 600 for eyebrows. The proven values: h1 at clamp(44px,6vw,78px), section h2 at clamp(32px,4vw,46px), body copy at 17 to 19px on 1.6 line-height, and headings pulled tight to 1.05 with -0.01em letter-spacing so the big Fraunces optical sizing feels carved rather than loose. Eyebrows carry the load of hierarchy at 12px, 0.22em tracking, uppercase, accent color, which lets headlines stay unornamented. The reason it works for a big-head-fat-feet brand: heavy display weight up top and a stable, plain body underneath is a literal typographic echo of the positioning, so the type system argues the concept without a single illustration.

FORGE WINNER, LAYOUT: Anchor everything to a 1180px wrap with 32px gutters (22px on mobile) and break the grid asymmetrically to avoid dead symmetry: hero at 1.05fr/0.95fr, split sections at 0.9fr/1.1fr with a reversed 1.1fr/0.9fr twin that swaps order via CSS order property. Fixed image heights (hero 560px, split 480px) give predictable rhythm, and a 4-column bento with grid-auto-rows:180px and a 2x2 hero cell (cell.a span 2/span 2) creates one clear focal block before the smaller cells resolve. The winning move is the low-horizon compositional logic carried into structure: generous headroom in the hero via 90px top padding, images that sit heavy at the bottom of their columns, and a closer that fades base into transparency top-to-bottom so the ground stays visible. Everything reads planted, never floating.

FORGE WINNER, MOTION: Keep motion cheap and physical. Reveal elements start at opacity:0 and translateY(40px), resolve over .6s ease-out, and stagger by a computed idx*100ms among reveal siblings, which produces a walking-in cadence rather than a simultaneous pop. Hover lifts are uniformly translateY(-2px) on buttons and translateY(-4px) on cells and glass cards over a .3s cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1), so interaction feels like weight shifting, not bouncing. Color transitions are near-instant (.02s) so hovers feel crisp while position eases. The marquee scrolls translateX(-50%) over 40s linear with a 2x duplicated track for seamless loop and pauses on hover. Critically, a full prefers-reduced-motion block kills all transitions, animation, and reveal offsets, which is why the build scores on both polish and accessibility.

FORGE WINNER, COPY: Obey the voice tokens literally: short declarative lines that plant both feet ("A big head. Two solid feet.", "Wide feet do not wobble."), concrete nouns over abstractions ("Feet: size 19. Head: no ceiling."), and zero banned words (no elevate, seamless, unleash, revolutionize, next-gen). The device that made this memorable is committing to the physical conceit everywhere, including invented-but-specific stats (14 years, 47.2%, 312 projects) and section labels that stay grounded ("The thinking", "The footing", "What we carry"). Every CTA names the concrete action ("See the work", "Start a project", "Bring the head. We handle the footing."). The lesson: a single controlling metaphor, applied ruthlessly from headline to footer copyright ("All feet on the ground"), reads as brand conviction rather than gimmick when the type and layout back the same idea.

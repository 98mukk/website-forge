The Story

RAISED ON STATIC
& STREETLIGHT.

Zebra makes music like a city remembers itself after dark. Born between the generator hum and the church bells of Surulere, he learned to find melody in the noise — the danfo horns, the FM crackle, the rain on aluminum roofs.

His sound is warm and electric at once: humid Afro rhythms wrapped in cold synthetic glass. It's the feeling of riding through Third Mainland Bridge at 2AM, neon reflections bleeding across the lagoon.

What started as voice notes recorded into a cracked phone became a body of work that now travels far beyond Lagos — yet never loses its accent. Globally polished, locally rooted.

"I want people in Tokyo to feel the heat of Yaba," he says, half-smiling under studio light. "I'm not exporting Lagos. I'm letting it leak out."

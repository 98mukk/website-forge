02 / LOGO LAWS

Construction and clearspace

The mark pairs a circle-in-square icon with a live-type wordmark. Clearspace unit X equals the diameter of the circle inside the "O" of the wordmark. Never render the wordmark as a flat image; keep it as live Sora type.

LAKE SHORE
White on dark. Clearspace X on all sides. Icon-to-wordmark gap = 0.385X.
LAKE SHORE
Black on light. Only two colorways ship: dark-on-light and light-on-dark.

Clearspace + partner lockups

  • X unit: the diameter of the circle in the wordmark "O". Derive all padding from X, never arbitrary pixels.
  • Horizontal lockup: X clearspace on all sides, icon-to-wordmark gap 0.385X.
  • Vertical lockup: X on all sides, vertical icon-to-wordmark gap 0.563X.
  • Partner lockups: separate our mark from a partner logo with a thin vertical divider and a 2X gap. Horizontal preferred, vertical as fallback.
  • Minimum size: full lockup 120px wide on screen, 24mm in print. Icon alone bottoms out at 24px.
Do not recolor or gradient the mark. Let the background carry color.
Do not place the wordmark on low-contrast surfaces.
Do not redraw, outline, or add a bevel to the icon.
Do not stretch, rotate, or squeeze the lockup.
Do not rebuild the wordmark by hand. Pull from official files.
Do not crowd the mark below the X clearspace minimum.

Light and dark surfaces

Dark is the primary environment. On the rare light surface (#F4F6F8), swap signal text for #0B0F14 and darken the accent to #0A9D68 to hold contrast. Never invert the accent to a pale tint.

Accessibility notes

Signal on Base clears 15:1. Accent #3DDC97 on Base reaches 9.2:1 and is safe for large text and UI, not body copy. Focus rings use the accent at full strength, always visible.

05 / IMAGERY

Art direction

Mood is quiet and premium: low-light architectural interiors and lakeside dusk lit with cool teal. Subjects are real workspaces and screens, never staged smiles.

Lakeside dusk, cool light
Low-light interiors
Screens and interfaces

Do shoot

  • Real small-business spaces at low light
  • Product screens with genuine data
  • Teal edge lighting on dark scenes
  • Wide crops with generous negative space

Never use

  • Stock handshakes or fake team high-fives
  • Purple gradients or candy color grades
  • Glossy 3D robots or mascots
  • Centered hero subjects staring at camera
06 / USAGE RULES

Applying the system

Keep the system calm. The accent earns attention by staying rare. When in doubt, remove color rather than add it.

Do

  • Keep the accent under 10 percent of any screen
  • Give sections real whitespace between them
  • Use live type for the wordmark, always
  • Show focus rings in full-strength accent
  • Design hover and active states, not just resting
  • Left-align hero subjects and headlines

Don't

  • Fill large areas with the accent as wallpaper
  • Add a second loud color competing with teal
  • Use faux-glass blur or cheap drop shadows
  • Apply gradient text on the hero
  • Add thin colored bars on dark cards
  • Use em-dashes or emoji in copy

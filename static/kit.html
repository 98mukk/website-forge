02

Logo Laws

The wordmark carries a weighted lower serif to echo the fat feet.

Broadfoot

Clearspace X = the diameter of the round "o" in the wordmark. Keep 1X clear on every side. Icon-to-wordmark gap = 0.385X.

Construction

Set the wordmark in Fraunces Black. The "o" is drawn as a true circle; its diameter is the unit X for all spacing math. Minimum digital width 120px; minimum print width 24mm. Below that, use the head-only monogram.

Partner lockups

Partner logos sit to the right of ours, separated by a thin vertical divider and 1X of clearspace. Horizontal lockup preferred, vertical as fallback. Black-on-light and white-on-dark applies to the whole lockup.

Don'ts

  • Do not stretch the feet serif to make it wider.
  • Do not recolor the mark. White or black only; let the surface carry color.
  • Do not place the mark on low-contrast tones.
  • Do not redraw or outline the "o" circle.
  • Do not add a drop shadow or bevel.
  • Do not crowd the clearspace with partner marks.

Imagery Direction

Grounded portraits, low horizons, generous headroom above the subject.

Mood & subjects

  • Warm neutral studio light, soft shadow under the feet
  • Subjects framed off-center with clear headroom
  • Low horizon lines to make the base feel solid

Treatments & bans

  • Full-frame focus, minimal retouching, real texture

06

Usage Rules

How the system stays upright across every application.

Do

  • Keep Base Linen as the dominant surface, 60 percent of any layout.
  • Reserve Accent Clay for links, headings, and one call to action.
  • Derive all logo padding from the "o" circle diameter.
  • Pair one Fraunces heading with a block of Work Sans body.
  • Let a partner logo breathe with a 1X divider gap.

Don't

  • Do not fill whole panels with Accent Clay as wallpaper.
  • Do not add a second loud accent to compete with Clay.
  • Do not render the wordmark as a flat image instead of live type.
  • Do not use thin colored bars on dark cards.
  • Do not stack Signal Pine more than once per view.
